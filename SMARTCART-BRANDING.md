# SmartCart — Branding guide

## Positioning

- **One-line:** SmartCart is your weekly meal planner, grocery list, and food log in one app.
- **For:** People who want to eat well with less decision fatigue, not “hardcore” tracking.
- **Tone:** Calm, practical, encouraging. Avoid guilt, crash-diet language, or “fix your body” framing.

## Name and writing

- **Product name:** `SmartCart` (one word, capital S and C).
- Avoid generic variants like “smart cart app” in headings; keep **SmartCart** as the brand.
- Pair with the core line: “Plan, shop, cook, track.”

## Visual identity on the website

- **Base:** Inherits the global NULL CLUB look:
  - Dark background (`--bg`), glass cards, and teal accent (`--accent`) from `css/styles.css`.
- **SmartCart emphasis:**
  - Use the main accent color for:
    - The “Get the app” button on the SmartCart page.
    - Links and section headings on the SmartCart page.
  - Keep the card copy focused on:
    - Planning the week.
    - Smart grocery list.
    - Cooking and logging in one place.

## Page structure (web)

On `smartcart.html`:

1. **Hero**
   - Title: `SmartCart`
   - Tagline: `Plan, shop, cook, track`
   - One-paragraph intro that matches the App Store description.
   - Primary CTA: `Get the app` (links to the App Store).

2. **Features**
   - Sections:
     - Plan your meals
     - Shop smarter
     - Cook with confidence
     - Track what you eat
     - More to help you stay on track

3. **Privacy**
   - Clear explanation that SmartCart is offline-first and stores data locally on device only.

This branding is already reflected in the SmartCart card on `index.html` and in the layout and copy on `smartcart.html`.
